<p><strong>The Antifreeze Of Automobile Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>CaCl2</li><li>CH3OH</li><li>C2H5OH</li><li>C2H4（OH）2</li><li>C3H5（OH）3</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The automotive antifreeze market includes various chemicals used to lower the freezing point of engine coolant. Calcium chloride (CaCl2) provides effective freezing protection and enhances corrosion resistance. Methanol (CH3OH) is a low-cost option known for its excellent heat transfer properties. Ethanol (C2H5OH) serves as a less toxic alternative, often blended with other agents. Ethylene glycol (C2H4(OH)2) is the most common antifreeze, offering effective freezing and boiling point suppression. Glycerol (C3H5(OH)3) is a biodegradable option, providing environmental advantages.</p></p>
