<p><p>The nonabsorbable sutures market is categorized into two main types: natural nonabsorbable sutures and synthetic nonabsorbable sutures. Natural nonabsorbable sutures, derived from biological sources like silk or cotton, offer reliable strength and biocompatibility but may carry a risk of infection. In contrast, synthetic nonabsorbable sutures, made from materials such as nylon or polypropylene, provide superior tensile strength, resistance to degradation, and reduced tissue reaction, making them ideal for various surgical applications. Both types serve critical roles in wound closure and tissue approximation.</p></p>
