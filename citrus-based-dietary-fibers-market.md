<p><p>The citrus-based dietary fibers market includes three primary types: pectin, hemicellulose, and cellulose. Pectin, commonly extracted from citrus peels, is valued for its gelling properties and is widely used in food products. Hemicellulose, a complex carbohydrate found in citrus cell walls, enhances dietary fiber content and offers health benefits. Cellulose, the most abundant fiber, provides bulk and aids digestion, making it essential for functional foods and health supplements. Together, these fibers contribute to the growing demand for healthy dietary options.</p></p>
