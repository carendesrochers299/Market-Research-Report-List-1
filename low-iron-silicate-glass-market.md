<p><ul><li>Rolled Glass</li><li>Float Glass</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The low iron silicate glass market comprises primarily two types: rolled glass and float glass. Rolled glass is produced by rolling molten glass into sheets, offering advantages in thickness and texture, making it suitable for architectural and decorative applications. Float glass, on the other hand, is made by floating molten glass on molten tin, resulting in a flat, smooth surface that provides excellent optical clarity and uniformity. Both types are valued for their low iron content, enhancing transparency and color neutrality.</p></p>
