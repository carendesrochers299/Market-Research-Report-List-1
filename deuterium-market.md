<p><p>Deuterium, also known as heavy hydrogen, is a stable isotope of hydrogen with one proton and one neutron in its nucleus, unlike regular hydrogen, which has no neutrons. It is naturally present in water as deuterium oxide (D₂O), often referred to as heavy water. Deuterium is widely used in nuclear reactors as a moderator, in scientific research, and in the production of semiconductors and pharmaceuticals.
