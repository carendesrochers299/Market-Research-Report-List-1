<p><p>Aluminum bronze alloys are primarily classified into two types: aluminum bronze and nickel aluminum bronze. Aluminum bronze consists mainly of copper, aluminum, and sometimes small amounts of iron, offering high strength, corrosion resistance, and excellent wear properties. Nickel aluminum bronze, on the other hand, incorporates nickel, enhancing its mechanical properties and further improving corrosion resistance, especially in marine environments. Both alloys find applications in marine, aerospace, and industrial sectors due to their durability and resistance to harsh conditions.</p></p>
