<p><p>Controlled-Environment Agriculture (CEA) encompasses various market types, including hydroponics, aeroponics, and others. Hydroponics involves growing plants in nutrient-rich water, allowing for efficient resource use and faster growth. Aeroponics, on the other hand, features plants suspended in air and misted with nutrients, promoting even greater efficiency and less water consumption. Other CEA methods include soil-based systems, vertical farms, and greenhouses equipped with advanced technologies. Together, these methods enhance food production in urban settings while minimizing environmental impact.</p></p>
