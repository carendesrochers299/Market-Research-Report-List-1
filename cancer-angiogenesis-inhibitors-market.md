<p><p>Cancer angiogenesis inhibitors are classified based on their specific targets in the tumor microenvironment. VEGF targeted therapies focus on inhibiting vascular endothelial growth factor, reducing blood supply to tumors. FGF targeted therapies inhibit fibroblast growth factor pathways, disrupting angiogenesis. Oncogene targeted therapies aim at blocking signaling from mutated genes that promote blood vessel formation. Matrix degrading and remodeling targeted therapies target the extracellular matrix to hinder tumor growth. Other therapies may include novel approaches like immune-based or small molecule inhibitors, diversifying treatment options.</p></p>
