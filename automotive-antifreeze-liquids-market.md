<p><p>The automotive antifreeze liquids market comprises various types, primarily propylene glycol, ethylene glycol, and glycerin. Propylene glycol is known for its low toxicity and is often preferred for use in vehicles where safety is a concern. Ethylene glycol, more common in traditional antifreeze formulations, offers excellent heat transfer properties but is toxic. Glycerin, a biodegradable option, is emerging due to its environmental benefits. Each type serves to lower freezing points and raise boiling points, ensuring optimal engine performance in varying temperatures.</p></p>
